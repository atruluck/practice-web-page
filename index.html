Important Terms
Term Definition Function
Artery A blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart. Delivers oxygen-rich blood from the heart to the body (except pulmonary artery).
Atrium One of the two upper chambers of the heart. Receives blood entering the heart: right atrium from the body, left atrium from the lungs.
Valve A flap-like structure that opens and closes inside the heart. Keeps blood flowing in one direction; prevents backflow.
Veins Blood vessels that carry blood toward the heart. Return blood to the heart: most carry oxygen-poor blood (except pulmonary veins).
Vena Cava The two largest veins in the body (superior and inferior). Carry oxygen-poor blood from the body into the right atrium of the heart.
Ventricle One of the two lower chambers of the heart. Pumps blood out of the heart: right to the lungs, left to the body.
